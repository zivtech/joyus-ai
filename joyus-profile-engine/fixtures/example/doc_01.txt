The proposed amendment to the administrative procedure fails to account for the practical realities facing affected communities. When an agency undertakes rulemaking that affects a broad class of individuals, the notice and comment period must provide genuine opportunity for meaningful participation.

Recent judicial decisions have reinforced this principle, holding that agencies must consider substantive comments and provide a reasoned basis for their final determinations. A cursory acknowledgment of public input does not satisfy the requirements of reasoned decision-making under established administrative law principles.

We urge the commission to extend the comment period and to conduct at least two public hearings in the affected regions before finalizing any rule changes. Affected parties deserve a process that is transparent, accessible, and responsive to their documented concerns.
