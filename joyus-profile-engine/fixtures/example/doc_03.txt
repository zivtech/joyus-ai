Our review of enforcement outcomes over the past fiscal year reveals a troubling pattern of declining penalties for repeat violators. When regulated entities commit the same category of violation within a five-year period, the applicable penalty schedule calls for escalating consequences.

The evidence demonstrates that the agency has frequently departed from its own guidelines without adequate justification. In case after case, entities with prior violations received penalties at or below the baseline amount. This approach undermines the deterrent purpose of the enforcement framework and signals to the regulated community that noncompliance carries minimal risk.

The consequences of inconsistent enforcement extend beyond individual cases. When penalties fail to reflect the severity and frequency of violations, public confidence in the regulatory system erodes. We recommend that the commission adopt binding penalty guidelines and publish annual enforcement statistics to promote accountability and transparency.
